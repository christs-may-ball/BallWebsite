FAQs
  • What is Christ’s May Ball 2020?

    Christ’s College traditionally holds a May Ball on the Tuesday of May Week following undergraduate examinations.
    This year our theme is ‘Paradise Lost’. Experience the incredible world of John Milton’s epic, stretching from the ethereal Fields of Elysium to the chaotic city of Pandaemonium

  • How much does the event cost to attend?

    Access ticket price: £100
    Standard ticket price: £160
    Queue jump ticket price: £170
    Dining ticket price: £190

  • What is an access ticket?

    Access ticketing is a scheme introduced this year, whereby Christ’s College students who claim a partial or full Cambridge bursary can purchase reduced £100 tickets.
    Access tickets will be confidentially verified by the Tutorial Office following ticket purchases. Individuals who incorrectly purchase a bursary ticket will be contacted to pay the remainder of their full ticket price.

  • How many guests am I allowed to purchase tickets for?

    Christ’s College students may purchase tickets for 2 guests, Guests are not required to be students at the University of Cambridge.

  • How do I pay for my tickets?

    Payments should be made via bank transfer to the account details listed in the email that you receive. You will have 48 hours to complete the bank transfer or the reference code will be invalidated and your ticket will be lost.

  • Can I change the name of my tickets?

    Yes. You can request a name change for your ticket(s) using your Raven log in on our ticketing system, up to 1 week prior to the Ball (Tuesday 9th June 2020). There will be an additional charge of £25 for any name change. Payment for name change will be made via bank transfer with a unique code as reference emailed to you after your name change request.
    A new ticket and QR code will be provided digitally to confirm name change. Access tickets for bursary students at Christ’s will not be eligible for a name change.

  • I have special dietary requirements, what shall I do?

    A variety of food and drink options will be provided at the ball, including comprehensive vegetarian and vegan options.
    Allergen information will be available at the stalls and on our app. For any further queries, please contact our Food & Drink team.
    Please fill out our dietary requirement questions following the purchase of your ticket.

  • What type of identification will I need to enter the ball?

    ID cards, driving licenses and passports are the only acceptable forms of identification. A Cambridge University card must bear the guests full name and date of birth (e.g. an undergraduate card).

  • When will doors open and close?

    Doors for Standard tickets will open at XX:XX.
    Doors for Queue jump tickets will open at XX:XX.
    Dining guests should report to the Great Gate at XX:XX.

  • What is the dress code?

    Black tie, White tie or national dress. Sports blazers are not permitted.
    The dress code will be strictly enforced. Those who are not properly attired will be refused entry to the ball.
